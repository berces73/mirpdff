Frequently Asked Questions

How is this different from Split PDF?

Split creates multiple parts from the whole document. Extract pulls selected pages into a single new file.

Is the original file changed?

No. A new file is created. Your original PDF remains unchanged.

Do extracted pages keep their order?

Yes. Pages appear in the order you specify.

Is Extract PDF Pages completely free?

Yes. This tool runs in your browser with no server cost. No registration required and no usage limits on the free plan.

Are my files private?

Browser-based tools never upload your files. For server-processed tools, files are automatically deleted within 1 hour.

What browsers support this tool?

All modern browsers are supported: Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge, and mobile browsers on iOS and Android.

💡 Pro Tips
  • Always keep a copy of your original file before processing.
  • For best results, ensure your PDF is not password-protected before using this tool.
  • After processing, verify the output by opening it in your PDF reader.
  • Need to reduce the output file size? Use Compress PDF afterwards.

When to Extract Pages

Pulling an Exhibit from a Contract
Legal agreements contain multiple numbered exhibits. Extract only the relevant exhibit to share without exposing the full agreement.
Archiving Individual Reports
Annual reports bundled into multi-year PDFs can be split into individual year files. Extract year-by-year for organised archiving.
Sharing Selective Findings
Research reports and audit findings often have confidential sections. Extract only the sections cleared for external distribution.

Pro Tips

  • Use comma notation for non-consecutive pages: 1, 5, 9, 15 extracts those four pages in that order.
  • Extracting pages creates a new file — original is untouched. Always verify the output before discarding the source.
