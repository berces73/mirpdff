Frequently Asked Questions

Can OCR read handwritten text?

No. OCR works on printed (typed or computer-generated) text only. Handwriting is not currently supported.

What scan resolution gives best results?

300 DPI or higher gives best results. Below 150 DPI, error rate increases significantly.

How accurate is the OCR?

95%+ on clean, well-lit scans. Skewed, faded, or low-resolution scans will have lower accuracy.

Can I use the output in Word?

Yes. Copy the text from the .txt output or open the searchable PDF and copy text from there.

How accurate is Turkish OCR?

On clear, well-scanned documents at 300+ DPI, accuracy exceeds 95% for Turkish text including special characters (ç, ş, ğ, ü, ö, ı).

Can OCR process multi-page PDFs?

Yes. All pages are processed in a single operation. Output is a single searchable PDF or text file.

My OCR output has many errors. What can I do?

Low scan quality is the main cause. Try scanning at higher DPI (300+), ensure good lighting, and select the correct language.

Pro Tips

  • 300 DPI minimum for reliable recognition. Most modern smartphone cameras exceed this — scan in good light, hold steady.
  • Select the correct language — Turkish OCR uses a specialised character model for ç, ş, ğ, ü, ö, ı. Selecting English for Turkish text causes frequent substitution errors.
  • Straighten pages before scanning — even 5° tilt measurably reduces accuracy.
  • For two-column academic papers, OCR may merge columns. Manually fix column breaks in the output text if needed.

OCR tips for best accuracy

  • Scan at 300 DPI minimum. 600 DPI for small text or complex layouts.
  • Flatten documents on the scanner — avoid curved pages or shadows.
  • Select the correct language before processing. Turkish and English give significantly different results.
  • For best results, scan in black and white — color scans add file size without improving OCR accuracy.

More Questions

Does OCR work on photos taken with a phone camera?

Yes, if the photo is well-lit, sharp and taken straight-on. Angled or blurry photos reduce accuracy significantly.

Can OCR extract text from tables?

Basic table text is extracted. Cell structure and alignment may not be perfectly preserved in the text output.
