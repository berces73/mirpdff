Frequently Asked Questions

Will the background cover existing content?

No. Background is added as the bottom layer — all existing content stays visible on top.

Can I apply a transparent background?

Yes. Set opacity to a low value for a subtle tint effect.

Can I apply different colors to different pages?

Currently, the same color applies to all selected pages.

When to Add a Background Colour

Brand Identity
Corporate documents presented in company brand colours signal professionalism. Add a subtle tint matching your brand palette to reports and proposals.
Draft Identification
A faint yellow or orange background immediately identifies a document as a draft — more visible than a text watermark for quick visual classification.
Presentation PDF Theming
Slide-deck PDFs exported with white backgrounds look flat. Add a light grey or off-white tint to match your presentation theme.
Accessibility Contrast
Some readers find pure white backgrounds fatiguing. A light cream or grey background improves comfort for extended reading.

Pro Tips

  • Keep opacity below 30% for background tints — too strong and text readability suffers.
  • Test on screen and in print — some colours that look subtle on screen print darker on paper.
  • Background is added as the lowest layer — all text, images, and annotations remain above it.
