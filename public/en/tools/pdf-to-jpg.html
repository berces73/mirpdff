Frequently Asked Questions

Can I adjust image quality?

Yes. Choose web quality (smaller files), medium, or high (best for printing).

Does each page become a separate file?

Yes. Each PDF page becomes its own JPG file. Download individually or as a ZIP.

When to Convert PDF Pages to Images

Social Media Sharing
LinkedIn, Instagram, and Twitter don't render PDFs inline. Convert your infographic, certificate, or report cover to JPG for direct posting with full visual impact.
Presentation Slide Export
Extract individual slides from a PDF presentation as JPG images for use in other presentations, websites, or documents — no PowerPoint required.
Document Thumbnails
Website and app developers generate PDF preview thumbnails. Convert the first page to JPG for use as a cover image in document libraries.
Email Newsletter Images
Email clients don't display PDFs inline. Convert your PDF brochure or flyer pages to images and embed directly in HTML emails.

Pro Tips

  • For social media, use medium quality (150 DPI) — sufficient resolution at smaller file sizes.
  • For print or large-format display, select high quality (300 DPI) to preserve sharpness.
  • If converting many pages, download as ZIP — individual file downloads multiply your clicks significantly.
  • PDF pages with transparent elements render with a white background in JPG. Use PNG output (where available) to preserve transparency.

MirPDF vs Other Tools

PDF-to-image conversion in MirPDF uses PDF.js rendering — the same engine powering Firefox's built-in PDF viewer — ensuring accurate colour and typography reproduction without server-side processing.
