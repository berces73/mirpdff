Why Convert PowerPoint to PDF

Universal Sharing Without PowerPoint
Not all recipients have PowerPoint or Keynote. A PDF opens on any device without software requirements — guaranteed consistent display.
Conference & Event Distribution
Handouts distributed at conferences, training sessions, and workshops are universally printed and shared as PDF — not live PowerPoint files.
Protecting Your Design Work
Sharing a PPTX file exposes your slide templates and design assets for easy copying. PDF protects your visual work while keeping content accessible.
Email Attachments
PowerPoint files are large and sometimes blocked by email servers. PDF versions are typically 50-80% smaller and universally accepted.

Pro Tips

  • Speaker notes are not included in the PDF output — export a separate notes PDF if needed.
  • Animations and slide transitions don't convert to PDF — each slide becomes a static page.
  • For handouts format (multiple slides per page), set this up in PowerPoint's Print dialog before converting.
  • If slide fonts display incorrectly in the PDF, embed fonts in PowerPoint before uploading (File → Options → Save).
