Frequently Asked Questions

Can I rotate only specific pages?

Yes. Enter page numbers to rotate only those pages, leaving others unchanged.

Does rotation reduce quality?

No. Rotation changes the page orientation metadata — no pixel re-rendering occurs.

Can I undo a rotation?

Rotate by the inverse angle (e.g. rotate 270° to undo a 90° clockwise rotation).

When to Rotate PDF Pages

Fixing Scanner Output
Flatbed scanners default to portrait orientation. Landscape documents — spreadsheets, architectural drawings, certificates — come out sideways. Rotate to correct in seconds.
Mobile Scan Corrections
Phone camera PDFs frequently capture pages at 90° or 180° due to holding orientation at time of capture. Batch-rotate all pages at once.
Mixed-Orientation Documents
Technical reports often include landscape diagrams within portrait text. Standardise orientation before sharing or printing.
Presentation Handouts
Slide decks exported to PDF are landscape. Recipients who print portrait-default get sideways output. Rotate to portrait for clean printing.

Pro Tips

  • Rotate all pages at once if the entire document is wrongly oriented — select 'All pages' rather than entering each number.
  • To undo a 90° clockwise rotation, apply 270° (three clockwise steps) — this is equivalent to one counter-clockwise step.
  • Rotation is structural metadata — zero quality degradation regardless of how many times you rotate.
  • Combine with Reorder Pages if some pages need rotation and others need repositioning.
