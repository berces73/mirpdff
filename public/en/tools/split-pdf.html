Frequently Asked Questions

Can I split every page into a separate file?

Yes. Select 'Split every page' to get each page as its own PDF file.

Does splitting change page quality?

No. Pages retain their original resolution and formatting.

Can I extract just one page?

Yes. Enter a single page number to extract it as a standalone PDF.

Does splitting affect the original file?

No. Your original file is unchanged. A new file is created from the selected pages.

Can I split a password-protected PDF?

Unlock the PDF first using our Unlock PDF tool, then split.

What happens to bookmarks and links after splitting?

Bookmarks pointing to pages outside the split range are removed. Internal links within the extracted range are preserved.

💡 Pro Tips
  • Use comma-separated page numbers for non-consecutive pages: 1, 3, 7-9 extracts pages 1, 3, 7, 8, and 9.
  • The 'Split every page' option is perfect for creating individual page files from a scanned batch.
  • After splitting, use Compress PDF if individual files are still too large.

When to Split a PDF

Extracting a Contract Addendum
A 40-page contract often contains multiple attachments. Split out only the specific exhibit or addendum you need to share without revealing the full agreement.
Separating Scanned Documents
Government offices and hospitals often scan multiple documents into a single batch. Split each document into its own file for proper archiving.
Sharing Presentation Slides Selectively
Share only the slides relevant to a specific audience — split the relevant page range rather than sending your entire deck.
Creating Per-Page Archive Files
Tax documents, receipts, and legal records often need individual files. Split every page at once with a single click.

Pro Tips

  • Use comma-separated ranges for precision: 1-3, 7, 10-15 extracts pages 1 through 3, page 7, and pages 10 through 15.
  • For large documents, split first then compress each part individually for optimal size.
  • Keep the original PDF — splitting creates new files, the source is never modified.
  • Pair with Reorder Pages if pages are in the wrong order before splitting.
